Meet the Team

Youth Policy Collective was founded by four high-school students who aspire for change.

Anant Singhal

American School of Bombay
Anant Singhal
Anant is a highschool junior at the American School of Bombay. An MUN enthusiast, he is passionate about international relations and economics. He has previously worked with the Quality Council of India and NITI Aayog on Unnati, a cutting-edge labour portal for blue and grey collar workers.

He has also worked with Stanford Angels & Entrepreneurs, a venture-capital organization run by Stanford alumni and xNav, a startup working in drone and aircraft navigation.

Aisha Tharaney

JNIS, Mumbai
Aisha Tharaney
Aisha is a junior at Jamnabai Narsee International School, Mumbai. She is an avid public speaker and MUN’ner. She has worked with several institutions, authored multiple research papers, interned at a law firm, moderated various discussions and debates and has also led her student body as the head girl of her school. An advocate for equal rights, she truly believes that our generation has the potential to achieve inclusion of the youth’s voice in leadership, policy and practise.

Manya Pratap Singh

The Shri Ram School, Moulsari
Manya Pratap Singh
Manya is a rising junior at the Shri Ram School, Moulsari. Being a passionate photographer, she has started her own photojournalism blog and is a part of her photography editorial board at school.

An aspiring psychology student, she recently worked with the UBC Social Identity Lab to explore the societal biases that lead to gender discrimination in the workplace. Additionally, She is working with the Naz Foundation to help young girls deal with barriers that keep them from achieving their goals such as lack of mobility, gender division of labour etc.

Ultimately, she aims to empower women to achieve their full economic potential and hopes to study behavioral economics in college.

Sarang Phulwaria

Our Advisory Council

Our expert panel of advisory council members who guide us in our endeavours.

Anjali Bansal

Founder, Avaana Capital
Anjali Bansal
Anjali Bansal is former non-executive Chairperson of Dena Bank where she successfully led the resolution of the stressed bank. She also chairs NITI Aayog Investment Council for Fintech and Women Entrepreneurship. Anjali is founder of Avaana Capital, a fund platform that invests in innovation led business models to achieve impact at scale.

She is on the Advisory Board of the Columbia University Global Centers, South Asia. She is a charter member of TiE, serves on the managing committee of the Indian Venture Capital Association, mentor to Facebook SheLeadsTech, NITI Aayog's Atal Innovation Mission She is a frequent speaker at forums like Harvard, Stanford, Columbia, IVCA, BSE and jury member for awards including ET 40 Under 40, Women Ahead, CEO Awards, VC Circle, AIWMI Wealth Awards and others.

Aditya Pittie

Owner, Pittie Enterprises Group
Aditya Pittie
Aditya Pittie is an entrepreneur, angel investor and mentor. He is the owner of the Pittie Enterprises Group - a diversified conglomerate with interests in precision automotive and aerospace component manufacturing, solar photovoltaic and solar thermal energy solutions, PayGo fintech platform, education consulting, real estate and angel investing.

Aditya graduated first in his class and holds a B.S.Engineering degree (Summa Cum Laude) in Materials Science and Engineering from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. He holds an MS/MBA degree in Management and Technology from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

Aditya is a mentor at the MIT Martin Trust Center for the Delta V program, and the Atal Innovation Mission of the Government of India. Aditya is a member of the University of Michigan Engineering Alumni Board and the Area Representative for the MIT Alumni Association in his home city. He is also the President of the Entrepreneurs' Organization (EO).
