International Relations

As our world becomes increasingly diverse, policy needs to be created which fosters inclusiveness and equity throughout society. Our Gender and Inclusion Council explores issues related to race, gender, sexual orientation, caste and age.


Gender inclusion is a concept that transcends mere equality. It's the notion that all services, opportunities, and establishments are open to all people and that male and female stereotypes do not define societal roles and expectations.

Anant Singhal

American School of Bombay
Anant Singhal
Anant is a highschool junior at the American School of Bombay. An MUN enthusiast, he is passionate about international relations and economics. He has previously worked with the Quality Council of India and NITI Aayog on Unnati, a cutting-edge labour portal for blue and grey collar workers.

He has also worked with Stanford Angels & Entrepreneurs, a venture-capital organization run by Stanford alumni and xNav, a startup working in drone and aircraft navigation.

Aisha Tharaney

JNIS, Mumbai
Aisha Tharaney
Aisha is a junior at Jamnabai Narsee International School, Mumbai. An avid public speaker and MUN’ner, she’s zealous about issues surrounding diplomacy and foreign affairs. An extremely passionate musician, she is the founder of a non profit that teaches underprivileged students how to play the guitar.

In the past, she has authored several research papers, interned at a law firm, moderated various discussions and debates, and has also led her student body as the head girl of her school. An advocate for equal rights, she truly believes that our generation has the potential to achieve inclusion of the youth’s voice in leadership, policy and practise.

Manya Pratap Singh

The Shri Ram School, Moulsari
Manya Pratap Singh
Manya is a rising junior at the Shri Ram School, Moulsari. Being a passionate photographer, she has started her own photojournalism blog and is a part of her photography editorial board at school.

An aspiring psychology student, she recently worked with the UBC Social Identity Lab to explore the societal biases that lead to gender discrimination in the workplace. Additionally, She is working with the Naz Foundation to help young girls deal with barriers that keep them from achieving their goals such as lack of mobility, gender division of labour etc.

Ultimately, she aims to empower women to achieve their full economic potential and hopes to study behavioral economics in college.

Sarang Phulwaria

Bombay International School
Sarang Phulwaria
Sarang is an 11th-grade student at the Bombay International School. He was elected as the Head Boy and was recently awarded the prestigious Citizenship Award by his school for his service to the community.
